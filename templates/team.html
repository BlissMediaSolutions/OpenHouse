Our Team

Paul Burgess

CEO

Paul Burgess - CEO

Qualifications: MA (Applied Theology Family Youth and Community Work)

Experience: Fifteen years experience in ministry to those on the fringe of society working for Churches, Not For Profits and Councils in the UK.

I love working for Open House and I am excited for the future. When I applied to work at Open House i saw the role as the coming together of all my previous roles & experiences. I find it very interesting to look back at my life and see how what seemed liked isolated projects coming together for my role at Open House. I firmly believe it is God’s plan for my life coming together.

I feel very privileged to be leading Open House into the next generation. So what are my hopes for Open House? In the short term my hope is that Open House will continue to serve the local community both in supportive and practical ways. Also that Open House will become better known in the Banyule area and that projects will be developed to serve the real needs of those living in the community. And that in another 40 years we will still be here.

Anne Rewell

Administration

Anne Rewell - Administration

Qualifications: Completion of Year 12

Experience: Secretarial, Administrative and Reception positions in Government departments, Private Enterprise and the Community Welfare sector

After working in many Secretarial/Admin/Reception positions in both private enterprise and government I decided that I would like to work for a community welfare type of organisation. I was looking for more than just a job - I wanted to make a contribution to people's wellbeing. I found what I was looking for at Open House and have been working in the position of Administration Coordinator for some years. I have enjoyed working with the staff, volunteers and participants and many good friends have been made along the way.

Many special memories come to mind but one that I am reminded of every year around Christmas time concerns my beginnings at Open House. I began working with the organisation only a few weeks before Christmas and one day I was walking down the side path returning to my office after lunch. Wednesday Club was preparing for our Christmas celebrations by practising some carols. Well, the music and singing that wafted out of the windows of the floor above the pathway was just beautiful; joyful and uplifting and they sounded like a choir of angels singing above my head. We have had many enjoyable Christmas's at Open House since that time but the memory of that special day stays with me.

Wendy Farrington

Program Manager

Wendy Farrington - Program Manager

Qualifications: Certificate II Community Services Support Work, First Aid

Experience: 20 years community work with semi/retired people, the lonely, those with learning difficulties and primary school children.

I have been involved with the Open House family since 1977, first doing voluntary work on and off over the years until God called me to join the staff in 2005. At first I ignored God because l had no desires to be part of the staff, l was happy plodding along doing my voluntary work. I was at church one morning when this overwhelming sensation came over me and it was God reassuring me that he wanted me to join the staff, and that he would be there with me every step of the way. I sunk into my seat and wanted to hide but knew that wouldn't do me any good because God knows where we are and everything about us. I give thanks and praise to God for calling me to be a part of this amazing ministry, to see lives changes and people grow is an incredible blessing.

My journey at Open House began in April 2014, when a worker from Volunteers of Banyule arranged for me to meet a guy called Glenn at the Ivanhoe site.
